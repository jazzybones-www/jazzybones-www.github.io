# reflections on an empty gas station

at 5:00 am this morning, i went on a run. there's something so sublime about
running at that time, the mostly empty streets make each car feel so much more
real. i feel a shared bond between me and the drivers at 5:00 am, as though we
are sailors, each on our own separate journeys through the emptiness, mostly
quiet and alone except for these few special moments. in just a few seconds, the
cars are gone, and i'm back on my own.

i passed a gas station, and paused. this gas station had been under construction
for well over a year, and had just recently finished. the bright, brand new
lights and vibrant colors from the fresh paint job stood in sharp contrast to
the silence and isolation. no cars, no pedestrians, just me and this hip, silent
gas station.

something in me finds immense beauty from this dichotomy. i don't know what it
is, some indescribable feeling of, something. not awe, nor fear, more like,
understanding. yes, that's the word, understanding. i understood that gas
station. like the chaos of the world had finally gone away, and i was left with
one thing to see.

soon, the sun will come up, and that magic will be gone. the chaos will return,
and the gas station will be just another thing to keep track of. but in that one
moment, without any cars in front of me or customers in front of it, i saw
something exactly as it was, without distractions.
