# the jazzybones word: malaise

i love the word "malaise". actually, i love every word - imho words are pretty
cool - but malaise specifically is a pretty good one.

there's this very specific feeling that malaise evokes in me. when you haven't
slept in over 24 hours and everything just feels heavy. you open your eyes as
wide as possible to fight the incoming exhaustion, it feels like you're fighting
harder than you ever have in your entire life just to stay alive, as though
you're fighting off death itself in a vicious battle within every second of
every minute of every hour of your long-lived yet soon-ending woken state.

maybe you start winning the battle. adrenaline dilates your pupils as you
suddenly feel like running five marathons in a row. you jump around and pace the
halls, mind racing at the speed of sound.

a few minutes later, it wears off. your heart pumps as fast as it can as you
forcefully pump oxygen into your lungs to feed your sleep-deprived muscles. your
feel each rapid heartbeat as you close your eyes and slowly recover.

an absolute truth approaches you, one which has approached you countless times
before, and one which will certainly approach you in the future. you understand
that your body lacks the elixir of sleep that it so desparately needs every day,
you understand that you've just been knocked out by a few minutes of running,
and you understand that for the sake of your survival, you _need_ to sleep.

you lay in bed, contemplating whether or not to brush your teeth and take a
shower. after much consideration, hesitation, and motivation, you manage to rip
yourself from the bed and complete your nightly routine. you throw yourself in
bed and go to sleep. the malaise always wins.
