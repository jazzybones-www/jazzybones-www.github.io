# the jazzybones word: juxtaposes

"juxtaposes" is one of those words that i only ever use in standardized tests.
it's such a versatile trick to put in an essay when you don't have anything else
meaningful to say about a text. if you can find literally any two different
ideas within a work, then you can say "the author juxtaposes the contrast
between A and B in order to show C, ultimately illustrating D."

that's really not the best way to do media analysis, you're supposed to start
with the text and figure out the tricks that the author used, but here we're
saying "i need to find one of these 5 really popular tricks that authors use"
and framing our text in order to make our argument work. to be fair, the text
probably _does_ use at least some of those 5 really popular tricks, and it
probably _does_ use them well enough to write a complete essay about. that essay
might even be halfway decent, but every single essay that you write for the
class will be halfway decent in the exact same way.

this is the big problem with standardized testing: it's standardized. standards
are great for charging cables and internet protocols, but standardized testing
creates standardized students who are good at standardized tasks. i have way
more fun writing on this blog than i ever have writing a timed essay, because
the crap that i write here is real, it's not some random bullshit i'm
deciphering from a 100 year old short story like hieroglyphics.

chatgpt writes a bunch of these really shitty essays way faster than a human for
way cheaper. it's inevitable that a decent chunk of the web is going to turn
into chatgpt generated slop. a decent chunk of the web has _already_ turned into
chatgpt generated slop. that sucks. don't use chatgpt to write your articles for
you, and please try not to be chatgpt when writing your articles.
