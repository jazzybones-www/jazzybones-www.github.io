# the jazzybones word: y'all's

ngl i thought i'd already written something for today. it's 11:45 pm and i'm
really cutting it close for time.

anyways, i used the word "y'all's" in writing the other day. like, "y'all's" is
definitely a real word, especially if you're a southerner speaking with other
southerners. like, "y'all" is a third person plural pronoun, and "'s" turns it
into a possesive. these are standard grammatical rules that exist in english,
but it creates a single word with two apostrophes, which feels illegal in
english.

this implies that over the years southerners have managed to create a logical
contradiction in grammar itself. like, either a word has at most one apostrophe,
or "y'all" can't be made into a possesive. obviously, "y'all" can be made into a
possesive, and the first statement is false.

this happens all the time in english. i before e except after c except in
"neither", "science", "eyeing", "feigning", "science", "society", and a whole
bunch of other words. according to my very crude regex matching on
`/usr/share/dict/words`, there are 4481 rules that follow this rule and 1024
exceptions.

that's all i have to say about this, i've now got three minutes to publish this
before i break my streak of writing to this blog every day for the past three
weeks.
