# i watched a Dhar Mann video, what happened next was unbelievable

a couple of months ago, before i started this website, i watched [this Dhar Mann
video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iufneMSmqSc), and it left me with the
ick. if you don't know, Dhar Mann makes feel-good inspirational short films on
Youtube. they're kind of infamous for having clickbait titles, which inspired
the title for this article.

anyways, the story of this video is that there's this kid that's failing school.
his genius older brother won the talent show last year by being a [human
calculator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mental_calculator), but the kid can't
even do basic addition. their parents give all the love to the older child and
send the younger one to a boot camp. the younger kid's first teacher is a
military man who yells at his students, but he's fired and replaced by a nicer
one. the new teacher discovers that the kid is actually dyslexic and just never
got the instruction that he needed, and that he's actually a genius at art. the
kid gets sent back to his family, they celebrate
[Diwali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diwali), and all is good. at the next
year's talent show, the older son does his math act, and the younger son paints
a picture while blindfolded (?!). the younger son impresses everybody, and the
parents proclaim that both of their children are geniuses.

i don't know any of these characters' names. they do have names, i just forgot
what they were and i'm not willing to rewatch the video to find out.

this really rubbed me the wrong way because, like, what if the younger son
wasn't actually good at art. what if it turns out that this younger child just
can't do math or art or science or music and is just doomed to be miserable at
the things that society expects him to be good at. maybe he's a really good
friend, but like, there are no friendship competitions. you can't get first
place at some friendship event and get a certificate that your parents can hang
on the wall to show off to all the other parents that your child is such a good
friend. also, maybe this kid is on the spectrum and people will never like him
unless he's constantly masking.

even if this kid was absolutely incompetent at everything he tried to do, he
would still deserve love and respect from his parents. this kid's dad treats him
like garbage throughout the entire beginning of the video. he openly expresses
his disdain for his own child, leading him (the child) to run away from home.
eventually, the dad sends the child away to some boot camp, never to come back
for the forseeable future, despite the child and wife's protests. this is not
ok. even if this child was a complete and utter failure at everything he did,
this wouldn't be ok. this only gets "resolved" after we find out that this child
is actually secretly a super mega art genius, but this just implies that if this
child wasn't good at art, he would still abuse his own kid.

it's the classic gifted kid trap. "you are special because of your abilities"
turns into "without your abilities, you are not special". this causes real harm
to a lot of people. that's a bad thing. i have no idea how to end this article.
