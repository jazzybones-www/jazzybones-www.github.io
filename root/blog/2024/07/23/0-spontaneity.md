# long term spontaneity

## i has to look up how to spell "spontaneity"

i've written in this blog almost every day for the past month and a half. i'll
admit, most of it is garbage. i never read the things on this website after i
write them, not even to check for typos. i haven't even visited most of the
pages in an actual web browser, i usually just publish and forget about it.

i treat this website more like a journal than a blog that anybody can read,
despite the fact that i write these articles as if i was writing for a general
audience (see: jb crypto, the phrasing of this paragraph). i've never been the
sort of person to keep a journal, let alone to write in it every single day. i
think this website is the largest collection of my writing that i've ever had. i
have had to keep journals for english classes in the past, but i always just
threw those away at the end of the term, and i wasn't nearly as motivated to
write in them as i am in this.

i don't know how this happened. one day i got depressed and started a website, a
couple of weeks later i wrote about quantization, and now i'm here. some spark
of spontaneity spawned in me and stayed for this whole time (that consonance was
unintentional).

that feels weird to me. i just added something to my daily routine. even when i
absolutely did not have anything to write at 9:00 pm on july 21, i wrote
anyways. i feel pressured to write here; even though this website almost
certainly has exactly zero readers, i feel bad when i don't have anything to say
to this imagined audience that i know doesn't exist. it's like that spark of
spontaneity is forcing me to write every day. something, anything, even if it's
not good, even if i can't get the wording just write and it comes out the wrong
way, even if i have absolutely nothing meaningful to write about, i feel this
immense pressure to just write.

sometimes i imagine that if i died today and...

actually, before i continue that thought, when i made this site, jazzybones was
supposed to be a separate character from the person that other people perceive
me as. i haven't been keeping up with that very well, but i kind of want to
bring it back. i don't want to reveal my real name on this anonymous blog, so
i'll say that the "real" me, as opposed to the jazzybones character, is named
Jinx.

anyways, sometimes i imagine that years after Jinx dies someone that knew them
would find this website. i wonder what that person would think of us after they
do. how would they perceive Jinx as a person, and how would these articles
influence that perception? most people who know Jinx would probably describe
them as a really energetic, upbeat nerd. Jinx loves music and dancing, and is
really good at math and computer science. if you've read a lot of this blog, you
probably see me as a depressed nerd that's really good at math and computer
science.

the "real" me, that is, my most authentic self, is probably somewhere in between
those two extremes. i genuinely do love just letting go and being as fun of a
person as i can possibly be, but i also have more sophisticated thoughts than
"Hey let's just have fun all the time!", but it's really hard to have those
conversations with people when i'm just known as the fun kid. it's even harder
when those thoughts could hurt the people closest to you, like opening up to
your mom about the emotional trauma she caused.

the real point of this website is to at least have a record of that other stuff.
i don't know why i feel the need to keep contributing to it, though.
