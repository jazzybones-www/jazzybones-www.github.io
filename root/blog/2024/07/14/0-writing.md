# writing is hard

when i'm writing, i feel like i have to have this clear narrative line of
reasoning. the reader reads this thing in a straight line from the top of the
page to the bottom, and in that process i have to have this smooth flow that
leads up to whatever ultimate idea i'm trying to present.

that's not how i think. i might have five completely unrelated reasons for doing
a thing. each of those might have some very lengthy tangent that's very
interesting but which would break the flow of the article.

in the [first jb crypto
article](https://jazzybones-www.github.io/blog/2024/06/23/0-algebra.html) i
dealt with this by providing a high level overview of the way that i think about
algebra (i.e., sets form groups form fields). then, in some very lengthy
foot "notes" i provided some extra info and proved a lot of the finer details
that would have made the high level overview a bit too complicated.

this makes the article a very nonlinear, but i think that the way that i wrote
it was closest to the way that i actually think. there's this clear linear
high level flow, and some clear linear subideas that connect to sections of that
high level flow, but connecting them all into a single linear thing would make a
very choppy article.

idk maybe i'm coping for being a bad writer.
