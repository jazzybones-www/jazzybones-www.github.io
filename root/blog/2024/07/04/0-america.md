# thoughts on america

america sucks right now. the supreme court has just ended abortion rights,
chevron deference, and has given the president an unprecedented level of
immunity, the presidential challenger is threatening to end american democracy,
and the voting system systematically prevents a real leftist movement from
forming without either massive reform or revolution.

it's a shame because i truly believe in the traditional american principles. i
believe in freedom for all, regardless of race, gender, or class. i believe that
a government should either serve the people that live under it, or shouldn't
exist at all.

i can find some solace in the fact that things have been worse in the past, and
that they always got better. slavery ended, women got the right to vote,
segregation was outlawed, and same-sex marriage was legalized. i also find
motivation in the fact that these changes took work. slavery took a whole civil
war to end, women's suffrage and the end of segregation took lengthy protests,
same-sex marriage took 56 years of fighting that started with the stonewall
riots.

american beliefs have stood for centuries, but they are constantly in danger,
and it's up to us to defend them.
