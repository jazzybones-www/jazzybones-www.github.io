# the jazzybones word: (homo/hetero)sexual

if someone is homosexual, that means that they are attracted to their own
gender, i.e. a man who is attracted to men, or a woman attracted to women.
likewise, if someone is heterosexual, that means that they are attracted to the
opposite gender, i.e. a man who is attracted to women, or vice versa.

to determine if someone is homosexual, heterosexual, or something else, you have
to know that person's gender, and the people that they're attracted to. these
requirements are kind of arbitrary, english could have evolved so that people
just referred to who they're attracted to without referencing their own gender
with words like "androphilic" (attracted to masculinity) or "gynephilic"
(attracted to femininity).

the idea that we have to know someone's gender to know their sexuality reflects
the idea that someone's sexuality is largely determined by their gender. this
isn't necessarily an incorrect or useless assumption, but it can cause problems
in some cases, especially with nonbinary people.

what's really interesting is that this idea is reflected in language itself. the
words that we use to talk about sexuality are reflective of certain assumptions
about gender and sexuality. this doesn't make these assumptions invisible or
impossible to overcome like with Newspeak in _Nineteen Eighty Four_, but they
can hide these assumptions or make them seem a bit more obvious than they really
should be.
