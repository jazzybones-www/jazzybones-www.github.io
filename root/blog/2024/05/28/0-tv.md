# the .tv tld

## cw: abuse

a tld is the last part in a domain name. there are tons of tlds, you've got
.com, .net, .org, and so on. many tlds are owned by entire countries. if you're
bri'ish, then you're probably familiar with websites like
[bbc.co.uk](https://www.bbc.co.uk/). the united kingdom gets the ".uk" tld, they
let a company called [nominet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nominet_UK) manage
.co.uk, and nominet gives "bbc.co.uk" to the actual bbc.

it may surprise you to learn that every two letter tld is owned by a country.
sort of. it's complicated. there's a standard list of two-letter country codes
called [iso 3166-1 alpha-2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO_3166-1_alpha-2),
and every entity on that list has its own tld. the list does include many things
that wouldn't normally be considered countries, for example io (as seen in
[jazzybones-www.github.io](jazzybones-www.github.io)) is assigned to the british
indian ocean territory, it's a pretty popular tld because i/o is a common term
in software development for "input/output".

these sorts of coincidences often obscure the relationship between a tld and its
country of origin. other noteworthy country-code tlds include .sh (british saint
helena, also a common [bigram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bigram)), .mx
(mexico, also stands for mail exchange), .me (montenegro, also just looks like
the english word "me"), and of course, .tv.

.tv is owned by [tuvalu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuvalu), an island
nation in oceania. because tuvalu's economy is so small and .tv domains are so
desirable, tuvalu's government gets around 8.4% of their income through the sale
of .tv domains (according to [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.tv)).

tuvalu's also in danger. rising sea levels are threatening to cover the entire
country in water. the situation is so bad that icann (the people who manage
domain names) drafted [a
policy](https://www.icann.org/en/system/files/correspondence/reynoso-to-botterman-28sep21-en.pdf)
to retire country-code tlds in the event that an entire country just goes away.

when i found out about this, i was just reading through wikipedia articles about
domain names. i was not ready for the existential dread that comes from learning
that we are literally just deleting an entire island. it just goes to show that
technologies are never truly isolated from the world around them. i've always
seen programming as a way to escape from the world around me. when i was in
middle school i would write code as a way to get away from my depression. i'd
always coasted my way through school, so when i actually had to work to do well
i began to crumble. this was made worse by abusive parents and teachers. i felt
like a failure in almost everything i did. everything except for software
development. i felt like computers were this solid foundation where i could just
make stuff and have fun without the pressures of the outside world forcing me
into this box that i couldn't fit into.

computer science offered this blissful escape from everything i was going
through, but it was just that, an escape. when i shut down my computer i still
existed in the world. i still had to face the mountain of homework, the
knowledge unstudied, the people who abused me.

the worst part about the entire situation was that i felt like it was my fault.
if i could just bring myself to do the things that i was supposed to do four
fucking hours ago i wouldn't be here. my teachers wouldn't hate me, my mom
wouldn't hate me, i wouldn't hate myself. the rational part of my brain knows
that this is bullshit. i was sleep-deprived, stressed, and had no real support
from my parents or teachers (unless you count making me feel like i was an
incompetent waste of space).

i've been thinking about that dichotomy a lot. on the one hand, i was the one
that didn't do my homework. on the other hand, my mother literally called me
"weak, both mentally and physically", and now i can't hear the words "mentally"
or "physically" without remembering that day. the best answer i've come up with
so far is that [no man is an island](https://allpoetry.com/No-man-is-an-island).
my actions are ultimately mine, but the decisions that i make are massively
influenced by a vast social network of people who influence people who influence
people who influence me. my depression was largely caused by my mother's abuse,
which influenced my actions and made me less inclined to do homework, which
caused my mother's abuse to get worse in an endless cycle of sadness.

that day when my mom called me "weak, both mentally and physically" was a big
turning point in my life. those words were the most painful thing i've ever
experienced by far. i just cried on the floor of my room for a several hours
before going to get some water. my mom was there, she said "sorry" like she did
every other time she'd yelled at me for the past year, and that was that.

the next day she apologized again and agreed to stop checking my grades
altogether, mainly for her own mental health. what do you know, my grades went
up. when i wasn't being verbally abused, i could actually bring myself to do the
work that i had to do. there's [a very relevant calvin and hobbes
comic](https://www.gocomics.com/calvinandhobbes/1988/05/24) which genuinely made
me sit down for several minutes and contemplate my life.

it's been many years since then. our relationship is still strained; on
principle i try to avoid spending time with her whenever possible.

computer science might be nothing more than an escape, but sometimes that's all
you have.
