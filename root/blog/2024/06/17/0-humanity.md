# the jazzybones word: humanity

obviously, as a ghost living in a computer, i am dead. i might write about the
way that i died later, but for now here's my closest call while i was still
alive.

it was the summer going into my junior year of high school. i decided to go on a
short bike ride, a couple of miles at most. i'd head around a mile away from my
house, turn around, and head back. i'd been on this route numerous times before,
and i didn't even bring my phone or water, since i knew that this would be a
short trip.

along the way i missed a turn and accidentally went a bit farther out than i'd
been anticipating. once i realized what i'd done i turned around and began
riding home.

this particular summer day was ridiculously hot. if i had to guess i'd say it
was around 105 degrees fahrenheit (around 40 degrees celsius). around halfway
back i began feeling the heat. i felt lightheaded, and each pedal felt like a
monumental task. i began seeing dots pop in and fade out of my view. at first it
was just a couple here and there but they quickly overtook my vision. i had to
actively focus on the sidewalk to know where i was going.

a bit farther there was around 100 feet (30 meters) of uphill riding. by the
time i reached the top i was dying. i'd stopped sweating, and i could barely
breathe. halfway up i had to get off my bike and start walking, and when i
reached the top i leaned my bike against the highway barrier and sat down on the
hot sidewalk. standing up felt like a massive effort. i was at an intersection,
so i pressed the cross button. several light cycles went by, and i realized that
i couldn't see the walk sign.

eventually, i waited for a red light and asked someone in their car for help.
they said "i can't hear you" until the light turned green and they left. i don't
blame them, i might have done the same thing.

a couple of minutes later, a fire truck pulled up and rescued me. multiple
people saw me strugglebussing (or strugglebiking) on the sidewalk and had called
the fire department. the person in that car that i stopped may have been one of
them. they took me to the station, gave me some water, and called my parents to
pick me up.

it's been a few years since that happened, and now i laugh at how dumb that
whole situation was, but a lot of things had to happen for me to survive that
day. some random drivers had to call for help after seeing some stupid kid dying
of heat stroke, some selfless group of heros had to respond to those calls and
actually save that stupid kid, and the infrastructure had to exist to connect
those random drivers and the fire department.

humanity is incredible. multiple people saw someone in need of help and took
some time out of their day to call the fire department. somebody received those
calls and physically went to my location to bring me medical care. on a much
larger scale, somebody built the cell tower that allowed the fire department to
get those calls for help, somebody built the truck that they drove out to pick
me up, somebody built the air conditioner that felt so cool when i finally got
out of the heat. in some way, billions of people put a tiny piece of their own
skills, talent, and labor to save my life, and i think that's beautiful.
