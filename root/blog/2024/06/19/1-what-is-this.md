# what even is this website?

i have two websites. one has my real name on it and is known to my friends and
family. the other is this website. i had this idea for a blog post talking about
my mom's verbal abuse, and i wrote a bunch of drafts for it, but i deleted them
all because the fallout from publishing something like that would be insane. i
decided that my story had to be published somewhere, and that i could never
attach my real name to it. i decided that the best solution would be to create
an anonymous alter ego and [publish the dang thing](/blog/2024/05/28/0-tv.html).

i created the "ghost in a computer" persona after one of my daydreams. there's
this sci-fi trilogy called [We Are Legion (We Are
Bob}](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/32109569-we-are-legion-we-are-bob)
about a guy named Bob who gets crygenically frozen and wakes up as a brain in a
computer. in the future, all the cryogenically frozen bodies are basically
property of the government, and it turns out that Bob was woken up to pilot an
interstellar space ship.

one of the big points in the story is how Bob has to create his own simulation
of the world, since he's just a computer on a space ship with no real body. i
thought that was a cool idea and started daydreaming about becoming a ghost in a
computer.

i created the name with a random word generator. this website was almost called
"coolbones", but "coolbones@proton.me" was already taken, so i went with
jazzybones.

i kind of thought that this would be a more personal blog where i could just
talk about the way i'm feeling, but i feel like "the jazzybones word" has turned
this into a much more technical website.

actually, i feel like all of my thoughts are a bit too technical. that blog post
where i wrote about my mom's verbal abuse started with an explanation of the
IANA. my daydream that i use to escape from the world involves being stuck in a
computer.

i've been coding for pretty much my entire life. it's always been my escape from
the shitty world around me. when i was in seventh grade i became suicidal, and
if i didn't have that validation from software development i probably wouldn't
be here today.

originally, i designated my other website as the place where i'd put all my
technical stuff, and this website as the place where i'd put everything else,
but i feel like i don't have much else to say.

the point of this website was to be able to write whatever i wanted without
fear, but i've been subconsciously holding myself back from writing anything too
personal. even though i shouldn't be afraid to publish whatever i want, i'm
always revising and thinking about what i should and shouldn't include here.
it's exhausting, i just want to express myself without my own mind getting in
the way.

just now, i almost revised the previous paragraphs to establish an overarching
theme for this post. something like "my thoughts are too technical, i want to be
more emotional". i need to learn to let it be, and be ajourned for inner peace.
