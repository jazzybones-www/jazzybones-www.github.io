# reflections on texas public education on slavery

i'm from texas. i like to think of texas as a cool nightclub in literal hell
being run by satan. like, the food's really good and all of your friends hang
out there, but we've also reached 100 degree temperatures for 41 days in a row
and the people in power are getting rid of abortion rights and pardoning
murderers because they were racist.

when i was in third grade, we had these history assignments where we'd read a
newspaper from the past and answer questions about it. they weren't real
newspapers, they were made by teachers to meet certain educational requirements
set by the state. i remember one of these newspapers was about slavery. it told
the story of two children in the 19th century, one from an abolitionist family
in the north, and from a slaveowning family in the south. in the story, these
are children, so they weren't like, eloquently arguing their disposition on this
complex moral issue, they were just saying what their parents were saying.

the northern child basically said "i think slavery is bad, but i've heard people
from the south saying that they need the money", and the southern child is
saying "we have two slaves and we treat them well, but the northern people are
saying that it's wrong." like, fuck that. you can't just pretend like there's
some nuanced middle ground between "yes slavery" and "no slavery" that's
objectively correct. like, imagine you're being held at gunpoint by two
gangsters. you start begging for your life "please, no! don't shoot me! i have a
family!" the gangster holding the gun to your head is saying "yeah fuck you i
don't care." suddenly, the ganster's sidekick says "hey now let's be reasonable.
i think you're both right in some ways and both wrong in some ways. let's try to
compromise and find a reasonable middle ground. how about instead of killing
them, you just cut their arms off?"

that would also be a bad outcome. the correct option is that the gangsters
shouldn't harm you at all. there is no compromise, there is no debate, you
shouldn't shoot people, and you shouldn't enslave people.

also, keeping in mind that this happened over half my lifetime ago, i feel like
i remember the arguments being weirdly biased towards the pro-slavery child.
like, fuck that.

in seventh grade, we were taught that the civil war was caused by a combination
of slavery, states' rights, and economic differences. like, yeah sure but it was
mostly the slavery thing. like, that had been _the_ political issue for half a
century before the civil war, the war was fought over the states' rights to do
slavery, and the economic differences were "we rely on slavery" vs "we don't
rely on slavery".

there's these subject tests that we occasionally have to take called the "staar"
(state of texas academic assessment of readiness) tests. in eleventh grade, we
have to take one on us history. the thing is that the test only asks you about
historical events from 1865 to the present, conveniently right after the civil
war and the end of slavery. the passing grade is also a 35% out of 100 for a 50
question multiple choice test.

the 1865 thing kind of makes sense. us politics changed a lot after 1865. we saw
the birth of the modern democrat vs republican system, reconstruction created
many civil rights reforms (haha get fucked [andrew
johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civil_Rights_Act_of_1866)), and the
industrializing us economy created massively wealthy men that ruled the country
such as andrew carnegie and john d rockefeller. all of those things would have
been unprecedented in the years before the civil war, so separating us history
into those two eras makes sense.

but like, a 35%? that's insane. i did the math, if you guessed on every question
completely randomly with a 25% chance of success with each question, you would
have a 4% chance of passing. that means that if everybody in a small classroom
just guessed their way through an entire test, at least one of them would
succeed. there are two essay equestions, but still, 35% is just too low.

conservatives are always saying "oh no the critical race theory, not the
critical race theory! they're indoctrinating our children!" but like,
conservatives, especially in conservative states like texas have an insane level
of control over the education system. a couple of years ago, my high school was
required to put up "in god we trust" signs in every building across campus. we
were almost required to put the ten fucking commandments in every classroom
before it was deemed unconstitutional. like, just let teachers teach, they know
what they're doing.
