# the jazzybones word: esquivalience

according to the 2001 new oxford american dictionary, "esquivalience" means "the
willful avoidance of one's official responsibilities". it's also a fake word. it
saw no usage in any english text prior to 2001, the people who wrote the
dictionary just made it up and put it in the newest volume. the idea was that if
you were to plagiarize the oxford dictionary, it would be really obvious if you
copied the word "esquivalience" without realizing that it wasn't a real word.

i feel like people rely on dictionaries and style guides to back up their exact
way of writing. like, sure, in an academic journal it looks nice if all the
articles look the same, but the rules of writing are really more like
recommendations. like, the people who write dictionaries aren't gods; when new
words are created, it's not like some guy working for merriam-webster goes "hey
i think that this would be a cool new word, let's make it a thing". words evolve
naturally, either as loanwords from other languages, as derivations of existing
words in a language, or some other strange process which takes place over many
years. dictionaries follow common parlance, not the other way around.

like, with the whole "water is wet" thing, the main argument on the side of
"water isn't wet" was that the one dictionary definition of "wet" had a slight
technicality in its definition, but dictionaries can be wrong. the fact that the
statement "water isn't wet" feels obviously wrong _makes_ it obviously wrong.
the word "wet" means exactly what we all feel like it means because if we feel
like it means something else then it would mean something else. i think the vast
majority of us feel like water is wet, so water is wet. even if the dictionary
says otherwise, water isn't wet. even if the dictionary says otherwise,
esquivalience isn't a word.

i used to be really hard on other people's grammar, but a few years ago i had
this moment of realization: it doesn't really matter. as long as i can get ideas
and concepts from my mind to yours through the medium of words, the way that i
do it is really just a technicality.

by the way, the people that make dictionaries acknowledge the fact that language
is constantly evolving and is mostly based on how we feel about it. like,
["rizz" is in the merriam-webster
dictionary](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/rizz). they don't really
care about language prescriptivists.
