# the jazzybones word: frisbee

the word "frisbee" is trademarked by Wham-O Inc. if you want to sell your own
circular aerodynamic objects, you can't call them frisbees. instead, you have to
call them "flying discs". "ultimate frisbee" and "frisbee golf" are legally
required to call themselves just "ultimate", and "disc golf".

in practice, everybody just says "frisbee". the fact that frisbee is a trademark
at all is seen as just a fun fact. there are [entire
articles](https://www.rd.com/list/surprising-trademarked-words/) just listing
words that are unexpectedly trademarked. the entire point of a trademark is to
make your specific business more recognizable, so the fact that trademarked
terms are being used completely generically is a bit ironic. i also think it's
kind of neat that despite the best efforts of Wham-O Inc. and the US Patent and
Trademark office, people continue to use trademarked terms entirely generically.
it's a little reminder that you can't control human language.
