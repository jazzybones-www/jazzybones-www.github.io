# the jazzybones word: x

"x" is an interesting letter. according to
[wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Letter_frequency), it's the third or
fourth least common letter in english, depending on how you're counting. it's
also seemingly used as a symbol just as often as it is a letter.

"x" could mean "the sound ecks", as in 'expression', 'fixture', or 'mixing'",
but it could also mean "cross" (as in "rail x-ing"), "kiss" (as in "love you!
xoxo"), "redacted" (as in "the year was 20xx"), "Christ" (as in "x-mas"), and
also as a generic symbol intentionally devoid of any meaning (as in "Malcolm X"
(he changed his last name to "X" to symbolize that he didn't know his own
ancestry, other than that he was African)).

the letter x blurs the lines between letter and symbol. like, there are 26
letters in the alphabet and one of them has been used basically like an emoji
for hundreds of years while keeping its original lexicographical meaning. i
think that's pretty cool.
