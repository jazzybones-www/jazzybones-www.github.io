# poem: one million, thirty seven thousand, one hundred dollars ago

<pre>
one million, thirty seven thousand, one hundred dollars ago
the internet was a far different place
fun but dumb but cool ideas roamed
across this beautiful, wild landscape

a city skyline of all that's good and bad
with human culture: casinos, greed, and sex
most of these ideas were just fads
most of these ideas are now dead

every pixel has a storied past
a business formed, rose, thrived, and fell
a massive effort that simply wouldn't last
though at the time it seemed to be quite well

although we seem to kick aside our dreams
new ideas form to fill the seams
</pre>

## non poetic waxing

this poem is in reference to [the million dollar home
page](http://www.milliondollarhomepage.com/). the idea was that you could buy
pixels within a 1000x1000 canvas to advertise your business. each pixel cost $1,
so theoretically the site could raise a million dollars.

the site became hugely popular, and demand for pixels was so high that the last
thousand were auctioned off for $38,100, which is where the title and first line
came from.

the site is absolutely chaotic. there are thousands of advertisements, mostly
for casinos, porn, and scams but also for some other stuff. it reminds me of the
giant walls of advertising that you see in many cities that are an absolute
eyesore to look at and which make the entire city just a bit more chaotic.
there's eomthing kind of beautiful about it, even if it is ridiculously
overstimulating.

the majority of the links on the site are dead. at some point, every single dead
link on that site was an idea. somebody was willing to pay $1/pixel to advertise
their brilliant website idea to the entire world in this really creative
experiment, and at some point in the past 18 years they abandoned it. a whole
city of businesses and ideas, gone and forgotten.

i wrote this poem after reflecting on this website. writing in here has become a
bit of a daily ritual, but at some point i'm going to run out of things to say.
i'll skip a day of writing, and then two, and then three. the days will turn to
weeks, which will turn to months, which will turn into an eternity as i begin
focusing on other things. eventually, Github pages will stop hosting this
website for whatever reason and this site will become a dead link just like all
the others on the million dollar homepage.

that's how the world works. things are created, they have some time in the
limelight, and then they go away to make room for the next generation. we could
mourn these websites all day, they're not coming back. let's instead appreciate
what we have now and accept that it'll be gone one day.
