# my college admissions experience

cw: rejection, anxiety, depression

february 1, 2024 was one of the most important days of my life. UT Austin
results came out on the same day that i had my MIT interview, and i was freaking
out. i was more anxious in the week before that day than i'd ever been for
anything else in my entire life. i was constantly filled with the urge to scream
into the void. one freezing night at around 3 am i couldn't take it any more and
biked around a mile away from my house where nobody would hear me and screamed
my lungs out. i'd pace around a single hallway over and over again just freaking
out and waiting for the minutes to pass by. i pulled an all nighter because i
physically could not bring myself to sleep, and then took a nap in a band
director's office the next day after being awake for over 32 hours.

when the day came i thought my interview went great. the person interviewing me
was super honest about how the entire system worked and i genuinely felt like he
wanted me to get in. i got home and checked my laptop. i got rejected from UT
Austin.

in the month and a half before results came out the anxiety continued. i stopped
eating, i would scream into a pillow in the middle of the night, i couldn't
sleep. i got rejected from pretty much every other school that i'd applied to
except the ones that i had automatic admission for. the MIT decision was just
another rejection on top of a growing pile. the Stanford rejection after that
was the final nail in the coffin for my hopes of getting into what i viewed as a
prestigious school.

when i was in seventh grade i became suicidal, and software development became
an escape from all the shit that i was going through in life. in the years that
followed i got pretty fucking good at it. i felt like i was destined for more
than what i got, which i hate saying because it makes me feel like a jackass.

for years now i've been developing opinions about universities from the things
that their alumni and faculty have done. Ron Rivest of RSA is a professor at
MIT, Donald E. Knuth went to Caltech and for a time was a professor at Stanford,
one of the inventors of Ethernet is a professor at UT Austin, and so on. i got
rejected from all of these schools.

i thought that i could be like them, but they said no.

i know that i shouldn't idolize random people who did some cool stuff one time
decades ago, especially when the universities that these people attend and teach
at stand to make massive profits from our tuitions, but it's so hard not to. i
just want to do something cool, but the people in charge didn't let me.
