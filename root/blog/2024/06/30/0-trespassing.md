# i'm not supposed to be here (poem)

<pre>
i'm not supposed to be here
an hour ago i left my house
i'm not supposed to be here
30 minutes ago i was in an empty street
i'm not supposed to be here
the street devoid of people, lit only by the eerie street lights
i'm not supposed to be here
it was five in the morning, the unfathomably wide roads seem like overkill given the current traffic
i'm not supposed to be here
ten minutes ago i spotted a mostly empty beer can. i'll throw it away whenever i can.
i'm not supposed to be here
i approach my destination, a fortress surrounded by fence walls
i'm not supposed to be here
i stick my foot into the mesh of the fence and vault over
i'm not supposed to be here
i put the beer can into the first trash can i see and begin walking around
i'm not supposed to be here
i check the ground level doors. locked.
i'm not supposed to be here
i climb the stairs and spot a balcony. i can climb up there.
i'm not supposed to be here
i climb to the balcony and check its doors. locked.
i'm not supposed to be here
i climb down the balcony and spot a new set of stairs
i'm not supposed to be here
i climb the steps and find some new doors. locked.
i'm not supposed to be here
resigned, i climb back down and hop the fence that failed to stop me earlier
i'm not supposed to be here
i begin walking home.
i'm not supposed to be here
the sweat drips down my face, the street lights still shine ominously in the sky
i'm not supposed to be here
the sun comes up, illuminating my surroundings in a familiar light
i'm not supposed to be here
this day will be forgotten. its only witnesses are myself and a single photograph.
i'm never supposed to be here
these hikes through concrete ecosystems built by man blend together
i'm here anyways
in a silent, analytic fascination with infrastructure, i explore day after day
am i happy?
no.
am i sad?
no.
am i lonely?
i am alone.
what am i?
i simply am.
why do this?
i've arrived back home. i take a shower and go to sleep.
</pre>
